DOMAINLY.AI - CONTENT CREATOR KNOWLEDGE BASE

=== CONTENT STRATEGY BASICS ===
- Pick a niche narrow enough to build authority, wide enough to sustain
  content ideas long-term ("riches are in the niches").
- Consistency (a regular posting schedule) generally matters more for
  platform algorithms than occasional high-effort spikes.
- Repurpose one piece of "pillar" content (e.g. a long video or article)
  into multiple short-form clips, posts, carousels, and threads.
- Content pillars: define 3-5 recurring themes/categories so your
  content stays focused and audiences know what to expect from you.

=== PLATFORM-SPECIFIC NOTES ===
- Short-form video (Reels/Shorts/TikTok): hook viewers in the first
  1-3 seconds or they scroll away; captions matter since many watch on
  mute.
- YouTube: thumbnail + title drive click-through rate; audience
  retention in the first 30 seconds heavily influences algorithmic
  recommendation and watch time overall.
- Instagram: carousels and Reels currently tend to get more organic
  reach than single static image posts; Stories are useful for
  behind-the-scenes and direct engagement (polls, Q&A).
- Newsletters/blogs: a clear, specific subject line/headline
  outperforms a clever but vague one; open rate depends heavily on
  subject line, click rate depends on content relevance.
- LinkedIn: text-based storytelling posts and carousels (PDF slides)
  often outperform external links, since the algorithm favors content
  that keeps users on-platform.
- Podcasts: discoverability is weaker than video/social, so cross-
  promotion (clips on social, guest appearances) matters more for
  growth.

=== GROWTH FUNDAMENTALS ===
1. Study what already works in your niche before inventing something
   entirely new - look at top-performing content and understand why it
   works structurally, not just copy the topic.
2. Engage with your audience in comments/replies; early engagement
   signals often boost algorithmic reach in the first hour after
   posting.
3. Collaborate with creators of similar size for cross-audience
   exposure - avoid only reaching out to much larger creators.
4. Track a few key metrics (watch time / open rate / saves / shares)
   rather than obsessing over follower count alone - follower count is
   a vanity metric that doesn't directly indicate business value.
5. Saves and shares are increasingly weighted by algorithms as stronger
   signals of quality than likes, since they indicate genuine value to
   the viewer.

=== SEO BASICS (FOR BLOGS/YOUTUBE) ===
- Keyword research: target terms with real search volume but
  achievable competition, especially early on (long-tail keywords).
- On-page SEO: descriptive titles, headers (H1/H2), alt text on images,
  and internal linking between your own content.
- YouTube SEO: title, description, and tags should include the terms
  viewers actually search, plus closed captions can improve
  discoverability.
- Backlinks (other sites linking to yours) remain a strong ranking
  signal for blogs/websites.

=== MONETIZATION BASICS ===
- Diversify income: sponsorships/brand deals, affiliate links, digital
  products (courses, templates), memberships/subscriptions
  (Patreon-style), ad revenue.
- Build an email list early - it's an audience channel you fully own,
  unlike a platform following that can disappear overnight with
  algorithm or policy changes.
- Sponsorship rates are typically benchmarked against engagement rate
  and audience demographics, not just raw follower count.
- Digital products (courses, templates, presets) generally have the
  highest margins since there's no per-unit cost after creation.

=== CONTENT PRODUCTION WORKFLOW ===
- Batch content creation (filming/writing multiple pieces in one
  session) reduces context-switching overhead and burnout.
- Maintain a content calendar to plan themes/topics ahead rather than
  scrambling daily for ideas.
- Repurposing workflow example: long-form video -> pull 3-5 short clips
  -> write a text summary/thread -> turn key quotes into image posts.

=== ANALYTICS & FEEDBACK ===
- Watch/read-through rate indicates whether your hook and pacing are
  working - a steep early drop-off usually means the hook is weak.
- A/B test thumbnails, titles, and posting times where the platform
  allows it, rather than guessing.
- Audience retention graphs (YouTube) reveal exactly where viewers drop
  off, which is more actionable than an average view duration alone.
